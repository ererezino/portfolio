Why I Walk

It started as medicine. It became something more.

Life Health Reflection

For the longest time, I had issues with my breathing. I never seemed to worry about it, even as friends joked about my "I can't breathe" comments in mildly stuffy or poorly ventilated rooms. It had also made my claustrophobia worse over the years.

Three years ago, I finally went to see a cardiovascular clinic and got myself checked. The doctors said nothing was wrong but recommended that I do more cardiovascular exercise. Simple enough advice. Since then, I've taken on walks and runs. The runs are few and far between, but walks? Walks have been great.

I didn't do a good job of tracking my walks in the first two years. I wore my Apple Watch a lot less back then. But last year, I was more consistent, and the numbers surprised me.

I climbed the equivalent of the Eiffel Tower 14.2 times. My best day was 27,700 steps. My average was 7,100 steps per day. All stats from my StepUp app.

In my apartment and every building I entered, I took the stairs more than the elevator whenever I could. That helped. I also had great walking days where I forgot my watch or it died halfway through, so I'm sure the real numbers are higher. But overall, I'm proud of this.

What I didn't expect was everything else that came with it.

The Unexpected Gifts

Peace of Mind
There's something about moving through the world at five kilometers per hour that quiets the noise in my head. No notifications. No urgency. Just one foot in front of the other.
A Photographer's Eye
Walking has made me more observant, more patient, more willing to stop and notice light falling on a wall or strangers sharing a moment they don't know is beautiful.
Navigation Skills
I used to be terrible at navigation. Now, I hardly forget routes. When you walk a city, you learn it in your bones. A place stops being destinations and becomes a continuous, knowable thing.
Better Breathing
Oh, and my breathing? It's fine now. Not perfect, but way better than three years ago. The thing that started all of this quietly fixed itself along the way.
I've come to love walks because of the peace they give me. There's something about moving through the world at five kilometers per hour that quiets the noise in my head.

I'm a hobbyist photographer, and walking has transformed how I see. It's made me more observant, more patient, more willing to stop and notice light falling on a wall or strangers sharing a moment they don't know is beautiful. I've taken so many pictures of the world around me simply because I was moving slowly enough to see it.

Walking also fixed something I didn't know was broken. I used to be terrible at navigation. I barely remembered routes, even ones I'd traveled many times. Now, I hardly forget them. When you walk a city, you learn it in your bones. You understand how neighborhoods connect, where the shortcuts are, which streets feel different at different hours. A place stops being a series of destinations and starts becoming a continuous, knowable thing.

What's Next

This year, I'm trying to run more and push my average to 10,000 steps per day. I'll let you know how that goes.

But even if the running doesn't stick, the walking will. That's why I'm sharing my steps data, to document the beauty of walking.
